# PianoSpark Curriculum Addendum: Gap Fills

## 1. Rhythm & Left-Hand Pattern Progression

Piano rhythm develops through left-hand patterns (the keyboard equivalent of guitar strumming). Each pattern is introduced when the learner has enough RH chord fluency to handle the cognitive split.

### Rhythm Levels

**R1: Whole Note Bass (Sessions 1–8)**
LH Pattern: Play root note, hold for 4 beats. One note per bar.
RH: Block chord (all notes pressed together, held)
BPM: 60–70
When: Levels 1–2
Why first: Absolute minimum LH demand. Learner can focus 90% on RH chord shapes. LH plays one finger, one note, once per bar.

Example in C-F-G:
```
LH: C...  |  F...  |  G...  |  C...  |
RH: CEG   |  CFA   |  BDG   |  CEG   |
```

**R2: Half Note Bass (Sessions 9–14)**
LH Pattern: Play root note on beat 1, play it again on beat 3. Two notes per bar.
RH: Block chord, one strum per bar
BPM: 65–75
When: Level 3 (Am, Em introduced)
Why now: Doubles the LH activity while keeping it the same note. Introduces rhythmic subdivision without adding pitch complexity.

Example:
```
LH: C . C . |  A . A . |  F . F . |  G . G . |
RH: CEG     |  ACE     |  CFA     |  BDG     |
```

**R3: Root-Fifth (Sessions 15–20)**
LH Pattern: Root on beat 1, fifth on beat 3. Two different notes per bar.
RH: Block chord or broken chord
BPM: 70–80
When: Level 4 (Dm, G7)
Why now: Introduces a SECOND pitch in the left hand. Root-fifth is the foundation of bass playing. The fifth is always easy to find — it's 7 white keys (or semitones) up from the root.

Root-fifth pairs (key of C):
- C chord: LH plays C, then G
- F chord: LH plays F, then C
- G chord: LH plays G, then D
- Am chord: LH plays A, then E
- Dm chord: LH plays D, then A

Example:
```
LH: C . G . |  A . E . |  F . C . |  G . D . |
RH: CEG     |  ACE     |  CFA     |  BDG     |
```

**R4: Root-Fifth-Octave (Sessions 21–26)**
LH Pattern: Root, fifth, octave, fifth (4 notes per bar)
BPM: 70–80
When: Level 5 (key of G, first black keys)
Why now: Four-note LH pattern gives the bass line movement and groove. Each beat has a note. Sounds "real" — like an actual piano arrangement.

Example:
```
LH: C  G  C' G  |  A  E  A' E  |
RH: CEG         |  ACE         |
(C' = octave above)
```

**R5: Broken Chord / Alberti Bass (Sessions 27–34)**
LH Pattern: Root-fifth-third-fifth (the classic Alberti bass)
BPM: 75–85
When: Levels 6–7
Why now: This is the pattern that makes piano sound like PIANO. Used in classical, pop, and everything between. Requires independence between hands because LH is now playing a different rhythm than RH.

Example:
```
LH: C G E G | C G E G | F C A C | G D B D |
RH: (held chord or melody)
```

**R6: Rhythmic/Syncopated Patterns (Sessions 35–42)**
LH Patterns — introduce variety:
- **Pop ballad:** Root (beat 1), chord (beat 2-and), root (beat 3) — "boom-chick-boom"
- **Swing feel:** Root (beat 1), fifth (beat 2-and, slightly late) — triplet feel
- **Latin/bossa nova:** Root (beat 1), fifth (and-of-2), root octave (beat 4)
- **Stride left hand:** Low root (beat 1), mid chord (beat 2), low root (beat 3), mid chord (beat 4)

BPM: 80–95
When: Level 7 (color chords)
Why now: By this point chord shapes are automatic. The learner can devote cognitive resources to rhythmic complexity.

**R7: Walking Bass (Sessions 43+)**
LH Pattern: Stepwise bass lines connecting chord roots
BPM: 75–90
When: Level 8

Example (C → Am → Dm → G):
```
LH: C  D  E  F | A  B  C  D | D  E  F  G | G  A  B  C |
```

Each note walks up (or down) the scale to connect smoothly to the next chord's root. This is the jazz/blues bass approach.

### Rhythm Integration Rules
- Never introduce a new LH pattern AND a new RH chord in the same session
- New LH patterns are practiced on the learner's most comfortable 2-chord pair
- Each pattern gets a minimum of 3 sessions before advancing
- Learner can always "drop back" to whole-note bass when learning new chords
- "Split Practice" always: RH alone → LH alone → hands together

## 2. Session-by-Session Lesson Plans

### Level 1: White Keys Only (Sessions 1–3)

**Session 1: "Hello, Piano"**
- Spark (1 min): Listen to "Let It Be" intro — just the piano chords
- New Move (5 min): Find middle C. Place thumb (finger 1) on C, middle finger (3) on E, pinky (5) on G. Press all three together. That's C major! Repeat 10x. Lift hand completely off keys between each press. Build the "drop and find" muscle memory.
- Song Slice (5 min): Play C major as a drone — press, hold 4 beats, release, press again. Play under "Row Row Row Your Boat" melody (just the chord, not the melody).
- Victory Lap (1 min): One clean C major chord. Record the sound.
- LH: Resting. Not involved yet.
- BPM: 60

**Session 2: "The Chord Machine"**
- Spark (1 min): Same clip — notice the chord changes now
- Review (2 min): Play C major 4x clean, lift between each
- New Move (4 min): Play C major in different octaves. Move the whole hand shape one octave up (C5-E5-G5). Move it one octave down (C3-E3-G3). Same shape everywhere! Introduce: "a chord is a shape, not a location."
- Song Slice (4 min): Play C major in rhythm — 4 beats on, 4 beats rest, 4 beats on. Metronome at 60 BPM.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Play C in three different octaves. Record.
- LH: Still resting.
- BPM: 60

**Session 3: "Both Hands Wake Up"**
- Spark (1 min): Listen for the bass notes in "Let It Be"
- Review (2 min): C major in rhythm at 60 BPM
- New Move (4 min): Left hand plays middle C (one octave below RH). Just one note, one finger (LH finger 5 or 3 on C3). Play LH C alone. Then play RH C chord alone. Then play BOTH together — LH bass C + RH C-E-G. Boom. That's piano.
- Song Slice (4 min): Play LH C + RH C major together, hold 4 beats, repeat. Get used to the coordination.
- Victory Lap (1 min): LH + RH together, clean. Record.
- LH: Single C bass note (whole notes)
- BPM: 60

### Level 2: The Neighbor Chords (Sessions 4–8)

**Session 4: "The F Chord (Your Hand Barely Moves)"**
- Spark (1 min): "La Bamba" clip
- Review (2 min): LH C + RH C major, 4 reps at 60 BPM
- New Move (4 min): Learn F major — BUT as 2nd inversion (C-F-A), NOT root position. Why? Because the C stays from the C major chord! Place RH: keep thumb on C, move middle finger E→F (one key up), move pinky G→A (one key up). Two fingers move ONE key each. That's it. Play C → F(inv) → C → F(inv).
- Song Slice (4 min): Alternate C and F every 4 beats with metronome. LH plays C under C chord, F under F chord (single notes).
- Victory Lap (1 min): Clean C → F transition
- LH: C (under C chord), F (under F chord) — whole notes
- BPM: 65

**Session 5: "The G Chord (Smooth Slide)"**
- Spark (1 min): "Wild Thing" clip
- Review (2 min): C → F(inv) transitions
- New Move (4 min): Learn G major — as 1st inversion (B-D-G). From F 2nd inv (C-F-A): thumb C→B (one key down), middle F→D (two keys down), pinky A→G (one key down). All stepwise! Play F(inv) → G(inv) → F(inv) → G(inv).
- Song Slice (4 min): The full C → F → G → C cycle using voice-led inversions. LH plays C, F, G, C bass notes. This is "La Bamba"!
- Victory Lap (1 min): Full C-F-G-C cycle, clean
- LH: C, F, G, C — whole notes
- BPM: 65

**Session 6: "I-IV-V Power"**
- Spark (1 min): "Twist and Shout" clip
- Review (2 min): C → F(inv) → G(inv) → C cycle
- New Move (4 min): Play the cycle continuously at 65 BPM. No pauses between chords. The goal: seamless transitions. Count out loud: "1-2-3-4, change, 1-2-3-4, change..."
- Song Slice (4 min): "Happy Birthday" — C, F, G. Play along slowly.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Continuous C-F-G-C loop, no hesitation
- LH: C, F, G — whole notes
- BPM: 65

**Session 7: "12-Bar Blues on Piano"**
- Spark (1 min): Blues piano clip — boogie-woogie feel
- Review (2 min): C-F-G cycle
- New Move (4 min): Learn the 12-bar blues pattern: C(4) → F(2) → C(2) → G(1) → F(1) → C(2). Count bars. This is the same structure as guitar but on piano it sounds completely different.
- Song Slice (4 min): Play 12-bar blues with backing track. Slow tempo.
- Victory Lap (1 min): One complete 12-bar cycle
- LH: Bass notes following chord roots
- BPM: 70

**Session 8: "LH Upgrade — Half Notes"**
- Spark (1 min): Listen to how the bass moves in a real recording
- Review (2 min): 12-bar blues
- New Move (4 min): LH upgrade: instead of one bass note per bar, play root on beat 1 AND beat 3. C . C . | F . F . | Same note, twice as often. Practice LH alone first (4 reps), then RH alone, then together.
- Song Slice (4 min): "La Bamba" with half-note bass. Sounds fuller!
- Victory Lap (1 min): Full song with half-note bass
- LH: Half notes (root on beats 1 and 3)
- BPM: 70

### Level 3: The Emotional Turn (Sessions 9–14)

**Session 9: "The Am Chord (One Finger Moves!)"**
- Spark (1 min): "Let It Be" clip — hear the Am chord
- Review (2 min): C → F → G cycle with half-note bass
- New Move (4 min): Learn Am (A-C-E). From C major (C-E-G): C stays, E stays, only G moves down to... wait, that's not quite right. From C root position: keep E, move C down to A, move G down to... Actually, Am root position shares C and E with C major! Play C (C-E-G) → Am (A-C-E). The C and E stay, only G→A moves. ONE finger. Mind = blown.
- Song Slice (4 min): C → Am → C → Am. Hear the emotional shift. Major to minor. Happy to reflective.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Clean C → Am transitions
- LH: C (under C), A (under Am)
- BPM: 70

**Session 10: "The Em Chord"**
- Spark (1 min): "Zombie" by Cranberries clip
- Review (2 min): C → Am transitions
- New Move (4 min): Learn Em (E-G-B). From Am (A-C-E): share the E. Move A→G (one step down), move C→B (one step down). Smooth! Play Am → Em → Am → Em.
- Song Slice (4 min): Em → Am → C chain. Three chords, all connected by voice leading.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Em → Am → C clean
- LH: E, A, C bass notes
- BPM: 70

**Session 11: "The Pop Loop"**
- Spark (1 min): "I'm Yours" or "Let It Be" — the four-chord song
- Review (2 min): Em → Am → C chain
- New Move (4 min): The legendary C → G(inv) → Am → F(inv) progression. The I-V-vi-IV. Practice the full loop. Use the voice-led inversions from Level 2 for F and G. This sounds INCREDIBLE with minimal hand movement.
- Song Slice (4 min): Loop the progression with backing track. LH plays bass roots.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Full loop, clean, with LH. Record this — it sounds like a real song!
- LH: C, G, A, F — half notes
- BPM: 72

**Session 12: "Let It Be"**
- Spark (1 min): Full "Let It Be" intro
- Review (2 min): Pop loop
- New Move (4 min): Map the specific "Let It Be" chord timing: C → G → Am → F, each chord for 2 beats (half bar) or 4 beats (full bar) depending on the section.
- Song Slice (4 min): Play along with "Let It Be" at 0.85x speed
- Victory Lap (1 min): Record the verse progression
- LH: Half-note bass
- BPM: 72

**Session 13: "Stand By Me"**
- Spark (1 min): "Stand By Me" clip
- Review (2 min): "Let It Be" progression
- New Move (4 min): Different order, same chords: C → Am → F → G. This is the "doo-wop" progression. Practice the specific transition F → G (the only slightly tricky one).
- Song Slice (4 min): "Stand By Me" play-along at 0.8x speed
- Victory Lap (1 min): Record
- LH: Half-note bass
- BPM: 72

**Session 14: "Level 3 Boss — Root-Fifth Bass Upgrade"**
- Spark (1 min): Hear how bass sounds with two different notes
- Review (2 min): Song of learner's choice from Level 3
- New Move (4 min): LH upgrade to root-fifth! LH alone: C then G (beat 1 then beat 3). Then A then E. Then F then C. Practice LH pattern alone for 2 min. Then add RH chords.
- Song Slice (4 min): "Let It Be" or "Stand By Me" with root-fifth bass. Sounds SO much more professional.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Full progression with root-fifth bass. Record.
- LH: Root-fifth pattern
- BPM: 72

### Level 4: Complete Key of C (Sessions 15–20)

**Session 15: "The Dm Chord"**
- Spark (1 min): "Autumn Leaves" clip
- Review (2 min): Pop loop with root-fifth bass
- New Move (4 min): Learn Dm (D-F-A). From Am (A-C-E): A stays, C→D (one step up), E→F (one step up). Voice-led! Play Am → Dm → Am → Dm.
- Song Slice (4 min): Am → Dm cycle with root-fifth bass
- Victory Lap (1 min): Clean Am → Dm
- LH: A-E (under Am), D-A (under Dm)
- BPM: 72

**Session 16: "The G7 Chord — The Magnet"**
- Spark (1 min): Listen for the "pull" — a chord that wants to resolve
- Review (2 min): Am → Dm transitions
- New Move (4 min): G7 = G major + F. Just add pinky to F key. Four-note chord: G-B-D-F (or voice-led: B-D-F-G). Play G7 → C. Hear the PULL? The F in G7 "wants" to move to E in C major. Satisfying resolution.
- Song Slice (4 min): Dm → G7 → C (the ii-V-I). THE most important 3-chord progression in all of Western music. Loop it.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Clean ii-V-I with root-fifth bass
- LH: D-A, G-D, C-G
- BPM: 72

**Session 17: "Jazz Day"**
- Spark (1 min): "Fly Me to the Moon" clip
- Review (2 min): Dm → G7 → C
- New Move (4 min): Extended chain: Am → Dm → G7 → C → F → ... → Am (circle back). This is how jazz standards move through chords.
- Song Slice (4 min): Simplified "Autumn Leaves" — Am, Dm, G7, C, F. Play along.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Full turnaround, clean
- LH: Root-fifth
- BPM: 75

**Session 18: "Hallelujah"**
- Spark (1 min): "Hallelujah" piano intro
- Review (2 min): Jazz turnaround
- New Move (4 min): "Hallelujah" uses C, Am, F, G — chords the learner already knows. Practice the specific sequence and timing.
- Song Slice (4 min): Play along at 0.8x speed
- Victory Lap (1 min): Record the intro/verse
- LH: Root-fifth
- BPM: 70

**Session 19: "Piano Man"**
- Spark (1 min): "Piano Man" intro
- Review (2 min): "Hallelujah" progression
- New Move (4 min): This song uses C, G, F, Am, Dm, G7 — every chord the learner knows in key of C! It's in 3/4 time (waltz). Practice counting in 3: "1-2-3, 1-2-3."
- Song Slice (4 min): Simplified "Piano Man" — chord changes with waltz bass.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Record
- LH: Root on beat 1, fifth on beat 3 (fits waltz time)
- BPM: 70 (waltz feel)

**Session 20: "Level 4 Boss — Root-Fifth-Octave Upgrade"**
- Spark (1 min): Hear full-range bass in a recording
- Review (2 min): Learner's choice song
- New Move (4 min): LH upgrade: Root, fifth, octave, fifth (4 notes per bar). Practice on C: C-G-C'-G. Then F: F-C-F'-C. LH alone first, then add RH.
- Song Slice (4 min): "Let It Be" with root-fifth-octave bass. Sounds like a real arrangement!
- Victory Lap (1 min): Record
- LH: Root-fifth-octave
- BPM: 72

*[Sessions 21–50 follow the same template for Levels 5–8, introducing new chords and progressing through the LH patterns as specified in the main curriculum.]*

| Sessions | Base BPM | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| 1–3 | 60 | Initial chord forming. Right hand only. |
| 4–8 | 65 | First transitions with inversions. Adding LH. |
| 9–14 | 70–72 | Pop loop mastery. Half-note bass. |

| 35–42 | 75–90 | Color chords. Broken chord LH patterns. |
| 43–50 | 80–100 | Graduation. Performance-ready tempos. |

### BPM Rules (identical to ChordSpark)
- When introducing a new chord: drop BPM by 10 from current comfort
- When introducing a new LH pattern: drop BPM by 10
- Never introduce a new chord AND new LH pattern in the same session
- Metronome always visible/audible
- Speed Challenge mode: current BPM + 10 for 30 seconds. Bonus XP.
- Learner can always override BPM

## 4. Onboarding & Placement System

### First Launch Flow

**Screen 1: "Welcome to PianoSpark"**
- "Let's find where you are so we can skip what you already know."
- Two paths:
  - "I've never played piano/keyboard" → Session 1
  - "I know some chords already" → Placement Test

**Placement Test (2 minutes max):**
- "Play a C major chord (C-E-G)" → (can't → Session 1)
- "Play an F major chord" → (can't → Session 4)
- "Play an Am chord" → (can't → Session 9)
- "Switch between C and Am at 70 BPM" → (can't smoothly → Session 11)
- "Play C → G → Am → F" → (can → Session 15+)
- "Play Dm → G7 → C" → (can → Session 21+, Level 5)

Self-report or MIDI detection: "Could you play that cleanly?"

**Screen 2: "Your Keyboard"**
- "What are you playing on?"
  - Full piano (88 keys)
  - 76-key keyboard
  - 61-key keyboard
  - 49-key keyboard
  - 25-key mini controller
- This affects: which octave range is suggested, whether bass notes are feasible with LH

**Screen 3: "Your Vibe"**
- "Pick 3 styles you want to play" (multi-select):
  - Pop, Classical, Jazz, Blues, R&B/Soul, Lo-fi, Rock, Gospel, Film/TV, Singer-songwriter
- Affects: which songs appear first, which backing tracks play, which LH patterns are prioritized

**Screen 4: "Your Schedule"**
- "When do you usually have 10–15 minutes?"
  - Morning / Afternoon / Evening / Random
- Sets default notification time

**Screen 5: "Let's Play"**
- One big button: "Start Your First Session"
- Done. No more decisions.

### Re-engagement After Absence

Identical to ChordSpark:
- "Welcome Back" screen showing last session and last chord
- Quick 5-minute refresher option
- "Comeback Kid" badge
- No streak guilt, no "you missed X days"

### Keyboard Size Adaptation

If learner selects a 49-key keyboard:
- LH bass notes may be limited — adjust to play in the same octave
- Root-fifth-octave pattern may not be possible — use root-fifth only
- Display only the available range

If learner selects a 25-key mini controller:
- LH bass is not possible — RH-only mode is default
- "You'll get the most from PianoSpark with a 49+ key keyboard, but you can still learn all the chord shapes with what you have!"

## 5. Visual Keyboard Specifications (for App Rendering)

### Keyboard Display

Each session should show a piano keyboard with:
- Full width of the relevant octave(s) (typically C3 to C5 for beginner content)
- **Active zone** highlighted: the keys currently in use are bright
- **Inactive zone** dimmed: all other keys are visually muted (60% opacity gray)
- **Chord tones** color-coded: root = primary color, third = secondary, fifth = tertiary
- **Finger numbers** displayed on or above active keys: 1 = thumb, 2 = index, 3 = middle, 4 = ring, 5 = pinky
- **Hand indicator**: a ghosted hand shape showing correct finger placement

### Voice-Leading Animation

When transitioning between chords, the app should animate:
1. Shared notes glow (stay in place, highlighted gold)
2. Moving notes show an arrow/trail from old position to new position
3. The distance of movement is displayed: "2 keys" / "1 key" / "0 keys (stays!)"
4. Aggregate score: "Total hand movement: 3 keys" (lower = better = smoother)
